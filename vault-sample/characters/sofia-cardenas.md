# Det. Sofia Cardenas

The 2024 frame's second chair — assigned to the "routine consult" with the
retired legend, young enough to have learned the Serrano case as folklore,
sharp enough to notice that the folklore has seams. She is the book's wager
that the theme isn't nihilism: truth and justice are not the same thing, but
someone has to keep the ledger of the difference, and Cardenas is who the
ledger passes to.

## Voice

- Precise, modern, lightly ironic — the register of someone fluent in both
  bodycam-era procedure and departmental theater. Her questions in the room
  are the reader's questions, half a page early. Use her to pace the
  interrogation scenes: when Cardenas leans forward, the reader should too.

## Function

- **The instrument that catches the narrator.** Vega's curated testimony
  survives every listener but her; she is the one who notices the S16
  canvass changing between tellings (ch. 4). She never calls it a lie.
  She writes down both versions. That notebook is the sliver of light.
- Mentee and witness at once: Vega teaches her, in the room, the way he
  thinks — and in teaching her, hands her the tools to take him apart.
  He knows. It's the closest thing to a confession he's capable of for
  most of the book.
- She is inside the hunt without knowing it (ch. 1–3): her diligence is
  being farmed by Crane's people to find Lucia. The moment she understands
  what her good work is *for* (ch. 5–6) is her arc's hinge.

## Contradictions

- Believes in the institution the way Brennan did — the parallel should be
  explicit enough to frighten the reader on her behalf. The question the
  book leaves open is whether knowing the Brennan story in advance is
  enough to save her.
- Ambitious, and honest about it; her career is real to her, which is what
  gives the final choice (memory over career) its price tag.

## Key scenes

- Ch. 3: she re-runs Vega's 1994 timeline on her own and finds the
  eleven-day gap nobody ever asked about.
- Ch. 7: Crane offers her the version of the case that comes with a
  promotion attached.
- Ch. 8 / frame close: what she does with Lucia's name, Vega's testimony,
  and her notebook. The ending's temperature is set entirely by this
  choice — write it last.

## Open questions

- OPEN: does Cardenas ever meet Lucia on the page, or only her file?
- OPEN: her exact final act — publish, prosecute, or preserve? Current
  lean: preserve, dated and witnessed — justice deferred, truth kept.
