# Lucia Serrano

The feared "killer" of the case — and the wronged party. Lucia is the heart
of the inversion: the murdered "victims" her hand found were the Ring's
monsters, and the monster of departmental legend was a sister doing what the
county paid other people to pretend to do. She was folding laundry when
Marisol got into the car. She has been trying to climb out of that laundromat
for thirty years.

## Voice

- Almost never on the page directly in 1994 — she exists first as absence,
  rumor, a figure in Vega's peripheral vision. When she finally speaks
  (late ch. 4 / ch. 5 in flashback), her voice is flat, exact, exhausted:
  a woman who finished crying years before the story starts.
- 2024 Lucia (glimpsed, hunted): grey, careful, ordinary. The horror is how
  ordinary. She stocks shelves somewhere inland. She has a cat.

## Contradictions

- The book's moral test: she is **right and she is not clean.** Working from
  a stolen case file and her own certainty, she read one man's alibi wrong —
  and a man who had touched no one went into the ground with the guilty.
  She knows which one. She can say his name faster than her sister's.
- She wanted the Ring exposed; her method guaranteed it never would be.
  Every man she took was a file that closed.

## Secrets

1. The count. How many, in what order, and the one that was a mistake.
2. That Vega found her in April 1994 — and let her go. What passed between
   them (one conversation, unrecorded, in the barranco) is the buried treaty
   the whole novel orbits. Neither has spoken of it since.
3. She knows who the Inheritor is long before ch. 7 — and did not stop them.
   Whether that is complicity, despair, or a bequest is for the reader.

## Key scenes

- Flashback, ch. 2: the laundromat — "I got a ride, sis" — the last ordinary
  hour of her life, told twice (once as memory, once as evidence).
- Ch. 4/5: the barranco conversation with Vega. The novel's true climax
  happened thirty years before the book's climax; this scene is why.
- Ch. 8: hunted, found — but not by the hunters. The sliver of light is what
  Cardenas chooses to do with her name.
