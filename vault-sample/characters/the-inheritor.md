# The Inheritor

Working name, deliberately unnamed in the notes — the reveal (~ch. 7) should
land as recognition, not introduction, so keep the identity fluid until the
present-day cast is locked. What is fixed: this is **Marisol's child**, a kid
in 1994, an adult in 2024, raised inside the case the way other children are
raised inside a religion — on Lucia's silences, on a mother who exists only
as a tended grave and a folder of newspaper clippings the county never
printed corrections to.

## Function

- Killer #2. The present-day death that "doesn't fit" (ch. 6, "A Different
  Hand") is theirs: same target class — the Ring's surviving heirs — but a
  different grammar. Lucia's work was grief with a method; the Inheritor's
  is doctrine. Second generation violence is always tidier and always
  colder. That coldness is the tell Vega reads.
- Thematically, the Inheritor is the bill for everyone's choices: the Ring's
  predation, the apparatus's burial, Lucia's method, **and Vega's silence.**
  Thirty years of the truth in custody produced this. The novel must not
  let Vega — or the reader — off that hook.

## Voice (once revealed)

- Fluent in the official record; quotes case numbers the way Brennan quoted
  the manual. The apparatus taught them everything, including its blind
  spots. They may work near it — records, dispatch, a courthouse — OPEN.

## Key scenes

- Ch. 6: the kill that doesn't fit — staged with details only someone raised
  on the original files would know, one detail *wrong* in a way only Vega
  would catch.
- Ch. 7: the reveal, in the same breath as Brennan's truth — the two
  betrayed children of 1994 (the believer's ghost, the victim's child)
  presented as one wound.
- Ch. 8: the confrontation is not an arrest. What Vega owes this person is
  the novel's hardest math.

## Open questions

- OPEN: gender, name, occupation — decide late, against whichever present-day
  character the reader is most inclined to trust.
- OPEN: did Lucia train them, tolerate them, or fail to stop them?
- OPEN: do they know Vega protected Lucia — is Vega a saint or an accomplice
  in their private catechism?
